## Farm Overview

Sunrise Dairy Farm operates a 500-cow dairy operation in Wisconsin, producing 4 million gallons of milk annually. Feed represents 55% of their operating costs, making feed quality and waste management critical to profitability.

## Challenge

The farm faced significant feed management issues:

- Frequent feed spoilage due to moisture intrusion
- Inconsistent feed quality affecting milk production
- Labor-intensive manual quality checks
- Temperature fluctuations in storage silos
- Difficulty tracking feed inventory
- $60,000 annual losses from spoiled feed

## Solution Deployed

We implemented an integrated feed monitoring system:

- Temperature and humidity sensors in all 6 silos
- Automated ventilation control systems
- Load cells for precise inventory tracking
- Mobile alerts for out-of-range conditions
- Historical data analytics dashboard
- Predictive maintenance alerts

## Implementation Timeline

### Initial Assessment (Week 1)
- Audited existing feed storage infrastructure
- Installed baseline monitoring equipment
- Measured spoilage rates and patterns

### System Installation (Weeks 2-3)
- Deployed solar-powered sensor nodes
- Installed automated ventilation controls
- Set up inventory tracking system
- Configured mobile alert system

### Optimization (Weeks 4-6)
- Fine-tuned temperature/humidity thresholds
- Established automated response protocols
- Trained staff on dashboard usage
- Created standard operating procedures

## Results Achieved

The system delivered immediate and sustained benefits:

- **40% reduction** in feed spoilage
- **$24,000 annual savings** from reduced waste
- **3% improvement** in milk production
- **85% reduction** in manual monitoring time
- **99.5% system uptime**
- **12-month ROI** payback period

## Operational Improvements

### Feed Quality Control
- Real-time moisture monitoring prevented mold growth
- Temperature alerts enabled rapid intervention
- Consistent feed quality improved herd health
- Reduced veterinary costs

### Inventory Management
- Precise feed level tracking
- Automated reorder alerts
- Better supplier negotiations with usage data
- Reduced carrying costs

### Labor Efficiency
- Eliminated manual silo climbs (safety improvement)
- Reduced daily monitoring from 2 hours to 15 minutes
- Staff redeployed to higher-value tasks
- Remote monitoring during off-hours

## Technical Details

The system includes:
- 18 environmental sensors (3 per silo)
- 6 automated ventilation controllers
- 6 load cell systems for weight tracking
- Solar power with 7-day battery backup
- 4G cellular connectivity
- Cloud-based data analytics

## Lessons Learned

Key success factors:

- Importance of proper sensor calibration
- Value of automated response systems
- Staff training critical for adoption
- Regular maintenance prevents false alerts
- Historical data enables continuous improvement
