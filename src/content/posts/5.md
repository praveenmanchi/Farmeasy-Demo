## Research Commitment

FarmEasy has partnered with several leading agricultural universities to advance **precision farming research** and develop next-generation sensor technology. Current research partnerships include:

- UC Davis: Studying optimal sensor placement patterns for different crop types
- Cornell University: Developing predictive models for frost damage prevention
- Texas A&M: Researching drought-resistant irrigation strategies using real-time data
- Iowa State: Creating soil carbon sequestration measurement protocols

### Areas of Collaboration

- Precision irrigation research
- Soil health monitoring methodologies
- Crop disease prediction models
- Climate adaptation strategies
- Sustainable farming practices
- Carbon farming verification

### Research Outcomes

- Published 12 peer-reviewed papers on precision agriculture in the past year
- Developed open-source algorithms for soil moisture interpretation
- Created educational materials for farmer training programs
- Established field trial sites across 8 states

## Praise from Academic Leaders

Research partners have praised FarmEasy's commitment to advancing agricultural science through:

- Real-world data sharing
- Equipment donations to research programs
- Joint grant applications for federal funding
- Student internship opportunities

## Impact Beyond Research

FarmEasy's university partnerships benefit the broader farming community by:

- Making research findings freely available to farmers
- Training the next generation of agricultural technologists
- Validating sensor accuracy through independent scientific study
- Developing region-specific best practices

### Get Involved

FarmEasy welcomes farmers to participate in research trials, contribute data to studies, or host field demonstrations. For more information, contact our research partnerships coordinator at research@farmeasy.com.
